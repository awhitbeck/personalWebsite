Development of Visual Aids

Together with an undergraduate student we developed a computer model of the cosmic ray detector. A MC program was written which could model the angular distribution of cosmic ray tracks as well as a visualization which will draw tracks passing through the detector and triggering the photomultipliers (denoted by red cylinders). The grey rectangles are representations of the four scintillators.



In order to engage kids in our demonstrations we allow them the chance to win prizes if they take a short trivia quiz in which all the answer can be found somewhere in the exhibit, one of which asks for an estimation of the number of cosmic rays which pass through ones body in a typical minute.



To help make the connection to the cosmic ray detector more explicit, we overlayed the animation of the detector on a cartoon person. The simulation of cosmic ray tracks were then drawn through both the detector and persons.
